4. Stop telling. Start listening.

When people of color start explaining to us about a racist incident, it makes us uncomfortable.

It makes us scared to think that we have something in common with someone who is racist.

We avoid this discomfort by deflecting the authenticity of the claim.

We think "well there may be two sides to this story" and that way we don't have to think "this person experienced racism from someone who fits a very similar profile to myself".

When a conversation about racism begins, it is our responsibility to give the oppressed the floor, and we take a peripheral roll.
